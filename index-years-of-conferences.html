WIP What I've Learned from Years of Conferences

Tech conferences = the tech world, just with more coffee and fewer bugs!

The Queue Algorithm

In tech, we optimize algorithms for efficiency. At conferences? FIFO: First In, Forever Outside.

Queues for registration, coffee, keynotes, lunch… it’s an infinite loop with no break statement!

The Food Buffet: A Study in Scarcity

Databases handle concurrent transactions. Buffets? Instant system crash.

  • Hesitate, and the best food is gone.
  • Buffet = caching system: Arrive late? Expired cache.
  • Balancing plate, drink, and utensils? Like managing system dependencies—one wrong move, and disaster.

The Coffee Break Economy

Coffee fuels tech people. Coffee breaks = memory allocation.

  • Too weak or strong enough to power a data center.
  • Networking happens because you're waiting for your fifth cup.

Keynotes vs. Real Work

Keynotes are like product demos: Inspiring, revolutionary… then you go back to debugging legacy code.

Swag Hoarding: The Loot Box Economy

  • T-shirts, stickers, socks—you take everything!
  • Suitcase = Museum of tech branding.

Networking: Pretending to Remember Names

Business cards pile up, but who is "Mike S."?

Conferences = Real-world LinkedIn: You collect connections, but never follow up.

Conclusion: The Real Lesson

Conferences = Tech world in real life.

  • Exciting, chaotic, full of innovation… and caffeine-fueled.
  • Plan your coffee break strategy wisely!

Thank you!
