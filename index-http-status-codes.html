WIP HTTP Status Codes IRL

The Comedy of Web Errors

Alex & Sam

Welcome! Today, we'll decode HTTP status codes with real-life, funny analogies. Get ready for a 2-person comedy show!

Introduction

  • Alex: The explainer
  • Sam: The skeptic
Alex: Welcome, everyone! Today, we're decoding the secret language of the web: HTTP status codes.
Sam: Or, as I call them, the internet's way of saying “Nope!” or “Maybe later.”

Successful Responses

200 OK

Everything's fine.
“Did you take out the trash?”
“Yep, all done.”

200 OK. The gold standard. Everything's fine.
Like when you ask your partner, “Did you take out the trash?” and they say, “Yep, all done.”

201 Created

Order a pizza, chef yells “Order up!”
Push a side project to GitHub.

201 Created. That's when you order a pizza, and the chef yells, “Order up!”
Or when you finally finish a side project and push it to GitHub.

202 Accepted

Request in the queue.
Boss says, “I'll think about it.”

202 Accepted. Your request is in the queue, but don't hold your breath.
Like when you ask your boss for a raise, and they say, “I'll think about it.”

204 No Content

“Any snacks left?”
Roommate just shrugs.

204 No Content. You ask, “Any snacks left?” and your roommate just shrugs.
Or when you text “?” and get no reply.

206 Partial Content

Netflix buffering.
Half a sandwich in the fridge.

206 Partial Content. Netflix buffering. You get half the episode, then… spinning wheel.
Or when you open the fridge and find half a sandwich.

Client Error Responses

400 Bad Request

Order coffee by yelling “PIZZA!”
Autocorrect fails.

400 Bad Request. You try to order coffee by yelling “PIZZA!” at the barista.
Or when you send a text with autocorrect fails.

401 Unauthorized

Bouncer says, “Name's not on the list.”
Cat denied laptop access.

401 Unauthorized. You try to get into a club, but the bouncer says, “Name's not on the list.”
Or when your cat sits on your laptop and gets denied access.

402 Payment Required

Paywall!
Subscription expired.

402 Payment Required. You try to read an article, but—paywall!
Or when your streaming service says, “Your subscription has expired.”

403 Forbidden

“Do Not Enter” sign.
Roommate's snacks: “Not yours!”

403 Forbidden. You see a “Do Not Enter” sign and try anyway.
Or when you reach for your roommate's snacks. “Not yours!”

404 Not Found

Look for your keys. Gone.
Click a link: “Page Not Found.”

404 Not Found. You look for your keys. They're gone. Forever.
Or when you click a link and get the dreaded “Page Not Found.”

405 Method Not Allowed

Pay with Monopoly money.
Microwave a metal fork.

405 Method Not Allowed. You try to pay for groceries with Monopoly money.
Or you try to microwave a metal fork.

406 Not Acceptable

Order vegan, get steak.
Quiet room next to drumline.

406 Not Acceptable. You order a vegan burger, and they bring you steak.
Or when you ask for a quiet room and get one next to the drumline.

407 Proxy Authentication Required

Friend changed Netflix password.
Sibling hid the car keys.

407 Proxy Authentication Required. You try to use your friend's Netflix, but they changed the password.
Or when you borrow your sibling's car, but they hid the keys.

408 Request Timeout

Text “Are you coming?”
Reply three days later.

408 Request Timeout. You text “Are you coming?” and get a reply three days later.
Or when you wait for your code to compile… and wait… and wait…

409 Conflict

Roommates both want the shower.
Edit the same Google Doc.

409 Conflict. You and your roommate both try to use the shower at the same time.
Or when you and your coworker edit the same Google Doc.

410 Gone

Café is now a gym.
Favorite meme page disappears.

410 Gone. You go to your favorite café, and it's now a gym.
Or when your favorite meme page disappears.

411 Length Required

Send package, forget address.
Form: “Name” field blank.

411 Length Required. You try to send a package, but forget to write the address.
Or when you submit a form and leave the “Name” field blank.

412 Precondition Failed

“If it's sunny, I'll go out.” It rains.
Python 2 instead of 3.

412 Precondition Failed. You say, “If it's sunny, I'll go out.” It rains.
Or when you try to run code that needs Python 3, but you have Python 2.

413 Content Too Large

Send 10GB file over email.
Fit closet into carry-on.

413 Content Too Large. You try to send a 10GB file over email.
Or when you try to fit your whole closet into a carry-on.

414 URI Too Long

Life story in a tweet.
Paste Google search link in chat.

414 URI Too Long. You try to explain your life story in a single tweet.
Or when you copy-paste a Google search link into a chat.

415 Unsupported Media Type

VHS tape in Blu-ray player.
.heic photo to old phone.

415 Unsupported Media Type. You try to play a VHS tape on your Blu-ray player.
Or when you send a .heic photo to someone with an old phone.

416 Range Not Satisfiable

Ask for 13th floor in 10-story building.
Scroll past end of webpage.

416 Range Not Satisfiable. You ask for the 13th floor in a 10-story building.
Or when you try to scroll past the end of a webpage.

417 Expectation Failed

Expect cake, get salad.
Expect code to work first try.

417 Expectation Failed. You expect cake at the party. There's only salad.
Or when you expect your code to work on the first try.

418 I'm a teapot

Coffee machine refuses to make tea.
Dog won't do your taxes.

418 I'm a teapot. You ask your coffee machine for tea. It refuses.
Or when you ask your dog to do your taxes.

421 Misdirected Request

Love letter to your boss.
Text “I'm outside” to wrong number.

421 Misdirected Request. You send your love letter to your boss by mistake.
Or when you text “I'm outside” to the wrong number.

422 Unprocessable Content

Doctor's handwriting.
Upload corrupted file.

422 Unprocessable Content. You try to read a doctor's handwriting.
Or when you upload a corrupted file.

423 Locked

Door locked, someone inside.
Edit file: read-only.

423 Locked. You try to open a door, but someone's inside.
Or when you try to edit a file, but it's read-only.

424 Failed Dependency

Can't bake cake: out of eggs.
Code fails: database down.

424 Failed Dependency. You can't bake a cake because you're out of eggs.
Or when your code fails because the database is down.

425 Too Early

Show up at 6pm, party starts at 9.
Deploy before tests finish.

425 Too Early. You show up to the party at 6pm. It starts at 9.
Or when you deploy before the tests finish running.

426 Upgrade Required

New game on old phone.
Browser says, “Please update.”

426 Upgrade Required. You try to play a new game on your old phone.
Or when your browser says, “Please update to continue.”

428 Precondition Required

Rent car, no license.
Buy beer, no ID.

428 Precondition Required. You try to rent a car without a driver's license.
Or when you try to buy beer without ID.

429 Too Many Requests

Hit elevator button 50 times.
Refresh page over and over.

429 Too Many Requests. You hit the elevator button 50 times.
Or when you refresh a page over and over.

431 Request Header Fields Too Large

Check in with 10 suitcases.
Text with 100 emojis.

431 Request Header Fields Too Large. You try to check in to a flight with 10 suitcases.
Or when you send a text with 100 emojis.

451 Unavailable For Legal Reasons

Show not available in your country.
Access banned website.

451 Unavailable For Legal Reasons. You try to watch a show, but “Not available in your country.”
Or when you try to access a banned website.

Server Error Responses

500 Internal Server Error

Kitchen catches fire.
App crashes, no idea why.

500 Internal Server Error. The kitchen catches fire. No food for anyone.
Or when your app crashes and you have no idea why.

501 Not Implemented

Smart fridge won't do laundry.
Button says “Coming Soon.”

501 Not Implemented. You ask your smart fridge to do your laundry.
Or when you click a button that says “Coming Soon.”

502 Bad Gateway

Order food through friend, they forget.
Slack down because AWS is down.

502 Bad Gateway. You order food through a friend, but they forget to tell the chef.
Or when Slack is down because AWS is down.

503 Service Unavailable

Customer support: “High call volume.”
Site down for maintenance.

503 Service Unavailable. You call customer support, and they're “experiencing high call volume.”
Or when your favorite site is down for maintenance.

504 Gateway Timeout

Ask friend's friend for salt. Never arrives.
Wait for reply in group chat… nothing.

504 Gateway Timeout. You ask your friend to ask their friend to pass the salt. It never arrives.
Or when you wait for a reply in a group chat, and… nothing.

505 HTTP Version Not Supported

PS5 game on PS2.
Modern site in Internet Explorer.

505 HTTP Version Not Supported. You try to play a PS5 game on a PS2.
Or when you try to open a modern website in Internet Explorer.

506 Variant Also Negotiates

“What do you want for dinner?”
“What do you want?” Infinite loop.

506 Variant Also Negotiates. You ask, “What do you want for dinner?” They say, “What do you want?”
Infinite loop. No dinner.

510 Not Extended

Trial software: feature not included.
Coupon expired last year.

510 Not Extended. You try to use a trial version of software, but the feature you need is “not included.”
Or when you try to use a coupon that expired last year.

511 Network Authentication Required

Airport Wi-Fi: log in first.
Zoom call: not signed in.

511 Network Authentication Required. You try to use airport Wi-Fi, but you have to log in first.
Or when you try to join a Zoom call, but you're not signed in.

Conclusion

The web is just as weird as real life.
And sometimes, just as broken.
May your requests always be 200 OK!
